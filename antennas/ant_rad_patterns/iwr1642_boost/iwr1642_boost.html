Introduction ============ IWR1642BOOST EVM antenna is designed to be operated in 77GHz to 81GHz frequency range. The use cases which can be addressed with this antenna are traffic monitoring, outdoor people counting, motion detection, perimeter security, surveillance etc. <img src="images/traffic_monitoring.jpeg" width="200" class="img-responsive"/> IWR1642BOOST EVM Antenna Picture & Dimensions ============================================= <img src="images/iwr1642boost_evm.jpg" width="400" class="img-responsive"/> <img src="images/iwr1642boost_tx_rx.png" width="400" class="img-responsive"/> The antenna peak gain of IWR1642BOOST EVM Antenna is about 10.5 dBi across the operating frequency band of 76 to 81 GHz. For more details about this EVM, please refer to the following link: http://www.ti.com/tool/iwr1642boost IWR1642BOOST EVM Stackup ======================== Please refer to below PCB stackup details for IWR1642BOOST Antenna. <img src="images/iwr1642boost_stackup.png" width="800" class="img-responsive"/> The mmWave layer is Rogers RO4835 with LoPro copper. ED (electro-deposited) copper is not a suitable material for mmWave frequencies. Detailed layout of this antenna with stackup are available for you to download and reuse at the following link: http://www.ti.com/lit/zip/sprr255 Simulated Radiation Patterns ============================ Simulated radiation patterns of IWR1642BOOST EVM Antenna for Elevation and Azimuth can be seen below. <img src="images/iwr1642boost_elevation.jpg" width="800" class="img-responsive"/> <img src="images/iwr1642boost_azimuth.jpg" width="800" class="img-responsive"/> In the above pictures X axis represents the angle (in degrees) and Y axis represents the gain (in dB).
